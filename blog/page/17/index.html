  • Product News: Define Results on Graded Quizzes

    Bucky Flowers · August 29, 2014

    Man, we are so excited to be releasing this feature. Not only has it been requested by a ton of our customers, we have very good reason to believe it will increase the share rates on your quizzes!

    The share rates on graded quizzes have been  lower than on outcome quizzes. We hope by letting you add custom result titles, descriptions, and result images to specific score ranges will make for a much more compelling reason for people to share!

    Here’s how it works:

    1. Create a graded quiz and click ‘Define Results’

     

    2. Add your results and define the score buckets. You can do that by dragging the handles to define the range or dragging from the middle to move the range to another score.

     

    Note: You’ll notice in my quiz there is a 50 percent gap between the lowest result and the next highest. You can define a ‘Catch-all result’ to fill in anywhere you don’t have score defined.

    That’s it! Depending on what score the user receives at the end, they’ll be given the coinciding result!

     

     

  • August 21st, 2014 Release – Retake, Take another quiz, Share suggestion

    Bucky Flowers · August 22, 2014

    This week we released a couple of features to help people find and take more quizzes and increase the quiz share rate.

    • Retake
    • Take another quiz
    • Share Suggestion

    Retake

    We added a button at the end of the quiz so that people can easily try for another score or outcome. We feel like people that get a favorable outcome or score are more likely to share their result.  If you’ve already taken a quiz we display your previous result on the start page and it  links to the result page so you can share it later.

     

     

     

    Take another quiz:

    At the bottom of the results page on Free, Standard, and Plus plans we are displaying other quizzes for people to take. Initially these are just randomly selected based on our most popular quizzes. Eventually these will be more based on user behavior and related to the quiz they just took.

    Share Suggestion:

    At the end of the quiz (on desktop only) we will pop up a  window to suggest that the user share the quiz if they haven’t already shared. The trigger for displaying this is if the user a) mouses out of the quiz or b) a few seconds after not scrolling.

     

     

     

     

     

     

  • An Alternative to an LMS

    Josh Little · July 24, 2014

    I’ve had the wonderful opportunity in my career to be a public school teacher, corporate trainer, small business owner, learning strategist for Fortune 500 companies, and creator of the world’s foremost enterprise social learning platform.  I’ve either used, tested, or been a reseller of all of the top learning management systems on the market today.

    When I was working with our clients at my first company, Maestro, they would come to us with a list of 900 features that their learning management system would need to do.  This is no joke…imagine a word doc with 16 pages of bullet points of “things our LMS needs to do.”  Being the dubious consultants we were, we would work diligently to find them the right solution that could slice, dice, chop, bake, rake, and mow for them.  In the end, we would setup the system for them and provide all of the necessary training for them to operate every one of those 900 features.  Then, we would send them on their merry way with their new system with every bell and whistle their heart desired.  Success, right?

    As I said, we were good consultants, so we would keep tabs on how well the system we built was working for our clients.  Because we wouldn’t hear much from them, we would check in with them from time to time.  What we found was that people would only be using 5 of those 900 “gotta have” features.  Although in theory all of those extra features made sense, in practice they just didn’t.  Against the backdrop of a busy day with deadlines and being pulled in a thousand directions, who has time to create a custom scheduled memorized report that emails a certain group when if/then criteria are met within certain parameters?  That stuff is useless in the real world.  What we found people really need to know is who took a quiz, when did they take it, how did they score, was it a passing score, and who didn’t pass.  That’s it.

    Qzzr is not an LMS….but it does have those 5 features that people actually use and need.  When we were contemplating building Qzzr, we knew that  most of the people using LMS’s out there today are only using it as a way to host quizzes and track scores.   I’ve personally seen it hundreds of times.   The content can be hosted anywhere, it’s really the quiz that needs to be tracked.

    We wanted to create a way for anyone to create a quiz in minutes and embed or link that quiz from anywhere and be able to track the scores.  This can be used by team managers who just want a simple quiz at the end of a presentation they are sending out.  It could be used by sales teams who want to certify all of their sales reps on the new product launch materials sent out.  It could be used by professors who just don’t want to deal with that clunky LMS.  It could be used by students as a way to create study materials.  It could be used by corporate trainers who want to offer a series of quizzes to their new training class.  Qzzr offers all of the useful features of an LMS in a simple, easy-to-use interface.

    And….you can’t beat the price

    As usual.  I wish you happy quizzing.

    Josh Little

  • Product News: June 23rd, 2014 Release

    Bucky Flowers · June 23, 2014

    We have made a number of enhancements that we are really excited about!

    Embed experience

    Previously, because of a limitation of iOS, we had to force quizzes to open in a new window on mobile devices. With this release we have found a way to keep the user on your site and have the best mobile quiz taking experience possible. On mobile devices, quizzes will open fullscreen. The user can close the quiz at any time and return to your site.

    On desktop, users will be scrolled to the top of the quiz if the top of the next question isn’t visible. All of these enhancements are only available with the <script> embed code… not the <iframe> embed code.

    To get all of this awesome embedded goodness, you will need to UPDATE YOUR EMBED CODE. Just go in to your quiz and copy the embed code and replace the old code on your site.

    See more information about embedding your quiz here.

     

    Standardized feedback location and “next question” behavior

    The location of the feedback was inconsistent and sometimes not visible on longer questions or on smaller screens. Now the feedback is fixed to the bottom of the window on all question types. In addition to this we wanted to reduce friction on finding a clicking the “Next” button. Now after answering an answer with feedback, the user will see their cursor turn into the next button and they can click/tap anywhere on the quiz to advance to the next question.

     

    Auto-Redirect feature

    We have had a lot of feedback from people wanting to embed a quiz on multiple sites, and have the shares return to the site that the quiz is embedded on. We have created an “Auto Redirect” feature. Now you don’t even have to think about the “Share return URL”. Just simply embed your quiz on any page, and when people share it, it will return to the site of origin automatically. If you do want to explicitly set where shares will go, add tracking parameters, or are using the iframe embed code, you can still use the traditional “Share return Url” in your quiz settings. If you do this be sure to turn off  “Auto redirect” for your embed code.

    As with the Mobile embed experience,  you will need to UPDATE YOUR EMBED CODE to get the auto redirect functionality. Just go in to your quiz and copy the embed code and replace the old code on your site. The old embed code still works, it just doesn’t have the new functionality.

    Get more details about redirecting shares here.

    Other Layout and Visual Updates

    We’ve made some design changes to the quiz start page. We have made the “Start quiz” button more prominent by making it solid white, and have a pulsing animation. On larger screens we have left justified the title/description and start button to showcase the background image more.

    On Qzzr in the top navigation bar we’ve expanded the page action buttons to show labels on larger screens for  clarity.

     

     

    On multiple answer (checkbox) questions we’ve made some changes to reduce friction and make interactions more clear. We moved the instructions “Select all that apply” to the top and made it larger. We also changed the action to say “Done” instead of “Submit” which was causing some confusion. Done implies that “I’m done selecting options.” Where “Submit” was causing many to think they had to submit each answer option that they selected.

     

    In the quiz editor, we expanded the the page action buttons show their labels for added clarity, broke out the appearance settings to simplify the settings dropdown, and added field labels to all quiz fields.

    There are also some slight stylistic changes to some of the interface components for consistency.

     

    We hope you enjoy the changes we’ve made, and as always we love to hear your feedback!
