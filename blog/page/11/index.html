  • These 3 Tips Will Help You Make Engaging Quizzes

    Connor Child · January 16, 2015

    My friend Jason made a great point in a post he wrote for Marketo. Here’s what he had to say about the popularity of quizzes over time: 

    “On an episode of Friends, Chandler walks out of the bathroom holding one of Rachel’s or Monica’s magazines.

    ‘All right, I took the quiz, and it turns out, I do put career before men,’ he announces to his friends.

    That episode aired in 1996.

    So although the quiz phenomenon may seem like a fairly recent trend, they’ve been around in some form for a long time. Magazines like Cosmo and Seventeen frequently contain quizzes about dating and relationships. Later, sites like BuzzFeed and Zimbio jumped on the concept to astounding results—this “What City Should You Actually Live In?” quiz from BuzzFeed has been viewed more than 20.5 million times.”

    Quizzes are now more popular than ever, and the best part is that it’s easy for anyone to get in on the action. Of course, we want everyone to succeed with their quizzes, which is why we’ve put together these three tips that will help you make an engaging quiz that people will want to take and share with their friends.

    1. Choose a topic that’s relevant to your intended audience. Superhero quizzes are always popular, but if you sell office supplies to office managers, you may need to tweak the topic slightly. Still want to create a “What type
    of superhero are you quiz”? Go for it, but consider wording it as “What type of office superhero are you?” That simple change can drive you forward as you develop the quiz. For example, one question might ask about weapons of choice such as document scanners or spare inkjet cartridges. This strategy requires a great deal of creativity. However, a creative quiz is highly likely to engage your audience and you have a better chance of getting people to share it with their friends.

    2. Use eye-catching photos. As always, choose photos that you have the rights to use so that you do not infringe upon anyone’s copyright. Try Flickr, making sure to choose photos offered under Creative Commons with commercial licensing. You can use photos as part of the quiz questions themselves or as interesting background visuals. You can also customize the share image. This is the one that will show up when people share your quiz on Facebook, so try to find one that will get people’s attention.

    Another quick-and-dirty way to find photos is to use Google Images, but make sure you are only using images that are licensed for reuse. So if I wanted an image of the sunset, for example, I would search “sunset” in Google Images, and then I would click on “Search tools.” Then, there is an option that says, “Usage rights,” and after you click on that, you can select “Labeled for reuse” and it will show all the images that are free to use.

    3. Come up with an attention-grabbing title. On Qzzr, you have a choice of graded and outcome quizzes. And in both cases, there are a few proven formulas for creating a title that capture people’s attention.

    For a graded quiz, here are a few ideas for titles:

    • Are you a ______ expert? (Example: Are You an Inbound Marketing Expert?)
    • Can you pass the ______ test? (Example: Can you pass the US citizenship test?)
    • The world’s hardest ______ quiz (Example: The world’s hardest car quiz)
    • Do you really/actually know the lyrics to ______? (Example: Do you ACTUALLY know the lyrics to “All About That Bass”?)

    For outcome quizzes, check out these title ideas:

    • What ______ are you? (Example: What Kind of Seamstress Are You?)
    • What ______ do you actually belong in? (Example: What 1984 flick does the quote belong to?)
    • What kind of ______ are you? (Example: What kind of introvert are you?)

    Engaging quiz takers is vital if you want them to take some sort of action after taking your quiz. Relevant topics, eye-catching photos, and attention-grabbing titles all go a long way toward engaging your quiz takers. Have fun, and they will too!

  • Create Online Trivia Quizzes Like a Pro!

    John Williams · January 16, 2015

    One of the reasons marketing quizzes have become popular has to do with the human tendency to show off. After all, it’s human nature to want to show others just how smart we are — and online trivia quizzes are the perfect way to do just that. They’re fun; they’re on interesting topics; and they’re relatively easy to create on platforms such as QZZR. Creating a trivia quiz is a fantastic way to let your quiz takers shine, and the possibilities are virtually unlimited.

    How to Create a Trivia Quiz

    Creating an online trivia quiz involves just a few steps:

    • Pick a topic
    • Align the topic to your audience’s interests
    • Do research
    • Create

    For some, topics for online trivia quizzes come easy. For others, that’s the hardest part. Below are a few popular ideas for trivia quiz topics:

    • Celebrities
    • Movies, TV shows, and pop culture
    • Fictional characters such as Harry Potter
    • Song lyrics
    • World War II trivia
    • Animals
    • History
    • Science
    • Vocabulary
    • Famous authors such as JRR Tolkien
    • Olympics
    • Sports
    • Myths
    • Religion
    • Geography
    • Criminals
    • Famous men and women
    • Famous quotes
    • Geography
    • Current events

    Virtually any of the topics above can be tailored to best meet your target audience’s interests. For example, if your target audience includes older men and women, you could create a pop culture quiz focused on celebrities or movies from the 1950s or 1960s. On the other hand, if your audience is made up of millennials, your pop culture quiz would be more effective if it is focused on current celebrities or movies. 

    Of course, if you can stoke the nostalgic flame that is in just about everyone, you’ve got a winner on your hand. For instance, check out this quiz about album covers from the 90s:

    Anyone who came of age during the 90s will have a hard time resisting that one.

    Aligning your trivia quiz to your audience’s interests is crucial if you want to get some engagement. Ideally, the quiz will also be relevant to your business or offer. For example, if you sell camping equipment, a movie trivia quiz centered on funny camping scenes could be effective. Try to find trivia topics that appeal to both your target audience and your company’s culture.

    Once you have a topic, writing the quiz is a simple matter of finding interesting trivia and entering your questions and answers into Qzzr. It can be fairly easy if you’ve done your homework, and fortunately there are a wealth of resources available online. For example, if you’re creating a trivia quiz on a famous actor or movie, IMDB.com is filled with fun facts. 

    Quick Trivia Question: Who played the President of the United States in the film Canadian Bacon? Quick Answer: Alan Alda. 

    Guess how long it took to research and write that particular question? Less than a minute.  

    Finally, create your trivia quiz in Qzzr. To make your trivia quizzes even more engaging, use plenty of images and animated gifs in addition to the text.

    Creating trivia quizzes is a fun way to interact with your audience, and it gives people the opportunity to shine. When they do well or receive a share-worthy outcome, not only will they feel good about the experience, they’ll also want to share their quiz results via social media. After all, we’re all show-offs.
