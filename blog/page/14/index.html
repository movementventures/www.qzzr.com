  • The Quiz That Has Generated $124,000 in Revenue (and Counting) [Case Study]

    Connor Child · December 15, 2014

    Last summer, my wife and I were heading out for a weekend at the lake. I had just bought a new pair of aviator sunglasses and was excited to try them out.

    Within one millisecond of seeing me with my new sunglasses, my wife said, “Absolutely not. You can’t pull off aviators.”

    I gotta be honest – that stung a little.

    Then, a few weeks ago, I came across a quiz from Zenni Optical that said it could help me discover the perfect frames for my budget and lifestyle. I was eager to see if this assessment would agree with my wife.

    If you must know, my wife was right – I didn’t get aviators. But, I did discover that I could pull off vintage-style frames. And it felt pretty good to show that to my wife.

    It turns out, there are a lot of people who want to know which frames are best for them. This quiz has been taken more than 140,000 times, but that’s not even the best part. Zenni was able to capture 7,000 email addresses, and the quiz helped generate $124,000 in revenue (and counting).

    Those results are nuts. They’re a testament to the creativity and ingenuity of the people out there creating quizzes. If you give your audiences content they love, they’ll want to buy what you’re selling.

  • Why People Love to Take a Quiz on Social Media

    John Williams · December 12, 2014

    As you flip through your Flipboard or scroll through your newsfeed, it often seems as though everyone is posting quiz results.

    “I’m Super Mario,” your cousin proclaims, and, apparently, your BFF is a “hot tamale.” The celebrity your niece is most like? Why, Taylor Swift, of course.

    But why does someone take a quiz?

    It’s all fun and games, but it’s also an effective form of marketing.

    The sheer popularity of quizzes on social media coupled with the ability to gather leads, position a special offer, or drive more traffic to a website combine to make quizzes a powerful marketing tool. While it’s fairly easy to see why marketers are using quizzes, a question worth pondering is this: Why are quizzes so popular on social media platforms?

    Here are a few thoughts:

    1. Quizzes are fun: They take about a minute to complete and another second or two to share. They’re often on topics of interest — can you say Game of Thrones? — and they give us a chance to test our knowledge.
    2. Quizzes are a great way to share information about yourself. Quiz results often say something about who you are and what you value, even if they are kind of goofy.
    3. Quizzes appeal to our sense of narcissism. We all want to look good and sharing flattering quiz results can do just that. After all, if someone, even if it is just a clever marketer, says that you’re most like Mother Teresa,  wouldn’t you want to share that?
    4. Quizzes are good conversation starters on social media. Your closest friends know all about your Game of Thrones fanaticism but your long lost BFF from high school may have no clue. After seeing your GOT quiz results on Facebook, now she knows. Oddly enough, she’s read all the books and has been binge-watching seasons one through four for the fourth time! Thanks to an online quiz, you find out you have something in common once again.
    5. Quizzes serve as a bite-sized distraction. Quizzes serve as a bite-sized distraction. All work and no play makes for a dull day, but who can afford to goof off all day long? A short quiz can be just enough of a respite from the daily grind without pulling you to far out of it. Taking a break and having a little fun is a proven way to get the creative juices flowing, too.
    6. Quizzes are a way to find out more about yourself. You may wonder how you compare to the so-called norm or if you’re making the best choices. Lifestyle and medical quizzes are a good way to see how you measure up or where you can improve. Best of all, they’re a non-threatening way to conduct an initial self-assessment.

    These are but a few of the many reasons quizzes are so appealing to users of social media. We probably missed a few. Feel free to add your own thoughts in the comments section below.

  • This Creative Quiz Was a Touchdown for CollegeHumor

    John Williams · December 12, 2014

    You’ve likely encountered your fair share of “Which Disney/Star Wars/Game of Thrones/Hunger Games/Fill-in-the-blank Character are You?” quizzes. These quizzes are indeed popular, but they are not your only choice. Another popular quiz type is the compare and contrast quiz.

    Examples of Compare and Contrast Quizzes

    What do we mean by compare and contrast quiz? Let’s take a look at a couple creative quizzes that use this technique as an example:

    NFL Player or Key & Peele Character – Key & Peele is a sketch comedy show starring Keegan-Michael Key and Jordan Peele. Their characters often had unusual names — as do many NFL players. This compare and contrast quiz asks questions such as:

    As the person taking the quiz, it’s up to you to figure out if the person in question is an NFL player or a Key & Peele character. This quiz is fun because some of the names are so unusual you think they couldn’t possibly be real. Surprisingly, seemingly fake names are real.

    Game of Thrones Scene or Creed Video – Both Game of Thrones and Creed videos feature dark and lavish scenery, making it difficult for even the most devoted of fans to distinguish between actual Game of Thrones scenes or a Creed video. Not only is this compare and contrast quiz fun because it appeals to fans of either franchise, it is also highly visual. It puts your knowledge to the test and challenges what you thought you knew you in a non-threatening manner. It also draws a parallel to the two different elements that you may not have noticed before.

    Benefits of Compare and Contrast Quizzes

    Compare and contrast quizzes offer several benefits:

    • Wide appeal – These online quizzes can appeal to two diverse groups as well. For example, NFL fans may have never heard of Key & Peele, yet they’ll likely check out the quiz. The same is true of the Game of Thrones Scene versus Creed Video quiz. Not only does this quiz tap into the huge Game of Thrones fan base, it also appeals to rockers.
    • Potential to go viral – Whether you scored poorly or aced the quiz, you’ll want to share it with your friends who share your passion for either Games of Thrones or Creed simply based on the subject matter alone. If you aced the quiz, of course you’ll want to show off. If you didn’t do so well, sharing a low score on an NFL Player vs. Key & Peele quiz isn’t nearly as embarrassing as flunking the US citizenship test.
    • Easy to write once you have the topic – Once you have the topic figured out, these quizzes practically write themselves. For example, let’s say you want to write a compare and contrast quiz such as “Fictional Hero or Real Life Hero.” Once you have that topic nailed, all you need to do is look up lists of both types of heroes and present them in your compare and contrast quiz. For example, John McClane (fictional) and John McCain (real life).

  • Online Personality Quizzes Can Work Magic for Marketers

    John Williams · December 11, 2014

    Which Walking Dead character are you? Which superhero are you? Which TV household should you be living in?

    Whether you’re a zombie, a man of steel, or could room with Monica and Rachel, these quiz types attempt to assess your personality. Personality quizzes remain one of the most popular quiz types on the Internet and in magazines. People love taking them and sharing their results.

    Are Online Personality Quizzes Accurate?

    If you have ever nodded in agreement when an online personality quiz nailed you as a pugnacious pug or a gentle giant mastiff, you may think that some personality quizzes are spookily accurate. Generally speaking, most people don’t take them too seriously; that is, they understand that they’re not true psychological measurements of personality.

    For example, check out this “What Classic Rock Band Are You?” quiz:

    It’s doubtful that any psychologist would extract anything out of whether you got Guns ‘N Roses or the Beatles. But in some cases, the outcome description may be a perfect representation of who you are, increasing the likelihood that you would share the results with your friends.

    How Do Quiz Makers Come Up with Personality Results?

    While some quiz content creators may use scientific principles to align outcomes with actual personality types, many simply take creative license.

    For example, there are 16 distinct Briggs Myers personalities. However, if you were to create a personality quiz centered on dog breeds, would you pick 16 different breeds and attempt to align each with one of the 16 personalities? Probably not. After all, online personality quizzes need to be short and simple in order to engage the people taking your quizzes. In all likelihood, you would probably be better off picking four or five diverse dog breeds such as a chihuahua, St. Bernard, and a mutt. The more distinct, the better.

    Likewise, would you refer to the Briggs Myers personalities when describing your outcomes? Maybe, maybe not. If you did, you might find inspiration such as “The Duty Fulfiller” (serious and quiet with well-developed powers of concentration – a working breed perhaps?) and “The Protector” (quietly forceful, original, and sensitive – a German shepherd or a doberman pincher perhaps?).

    Quiz creators get their inspiration from any number of sources including science, movies, and examples from real life. Simply look around your office, neighborhood, or community and you’ll see all kinds of personalities. Within different groups, you’ll likely see any number of personalities that are not scientifically documented but are real nonetheless. Virtually any group you can think of will have its own personality types. Below are just a few examples of personality quiz ideas:

    • What type of mom are you?
    • What type of fitness buff are you?
    • What type if musician are you?
    • What type of high school sports coach are you?
    • What type of boss are you?
    • What type of librarian are you?
    • What type of sports fan are you?

    Why are Online Personality Quizzes Good for Marketers?

    Creating an online personality quiz is good for marketers for several reasons:

    • They are popular; thus, they have the potential to go viral. People enjoy taking personality quizzes, and they like sharing flattering or funny results. When a quiz gets shared, others are exposed to it and prompted to take the quiz for themselves. You can expect results based on sheer numbers. The more targeted and optimized the quiz is, the better its results.
    • Online personality quizzes can be used to drive traffic to a website, generate leads, or present special offers. For example, if you embed the quiz into your website and people share their results with their social media followers, those interested in taking the quiz for themselves will need to visit your website in order to do so. This may be their first visit to your site where you have the opportunity to begin a relationship.Not only can you use quizzes to drive traffic to a specific site, you could also prompt users to give you their email addresses at the end of the quiz or present a special offer as a thank you for taking the quiz. You could even offer different offers based on the quiz results if desired.
    • Online personality quizzes could be used to segment leads based on outcomes. Would you like to know which members of your email marketing list consider themselves confident self-starters and which ones feel timid and nervous? This knowledge could be helpful in presenting special offers in the future. A personality quiz is an effective way of identifying specific traits and segmenting your list accordingly.

  • Time Crunch? Here’s How to Outsource Your Quiz Creation Like a Pro

    John Williams · December 9, 2014

    With the undeniable popularity of quizzes, they have become a fantastic tool for generating leads, presenting special offers, and driving social traffic. If you build them, the leads will come.

    This brings up a question: who should write your quizzes? Should you write them yourself? Let an intern do it? Hire a freelancer? Work with an agency? Here’s a look at the various options available along with their advantages and disadvantages.

    Write your own quiz content

    With the right quiz creation platform, writing your own quiz content isn’t overly difficult or time-consuming.  For example, QZZR practically walks you through quiz creation.

    Advantages. No one has a better understanding of your target audience’s likes, dislikes, needs, problems, and interests than you.

    Disadvantages. You may not have time to spare for additional marketing tasks. You may not feel particularly creative or able to commit to publishing quizzes on a regular basis.

    Assign quiz content creation to a member of your team

    If a member of your team has been asking you for more creative freedom, assigning quiz content creation to that team member could be a win-win scenario.

    Advantages. You keep creative control within your company, the other team member is able to contribute in a fun and meaningful manner, and the content is likely to be relevant to your target audience due to your team member’s solid understanding of your company and who it serves.

    Disadvantages. The new task owner may face the same challenges you faced such as little time for extra work and the inability to commit to publishing according to a regular schedule. In addition, the novelty may wear off or the creative juices dry up after creating a few quizzes.

    Have an intern create quizzes

    Marketing interns are eager to please, and they may enjoy the creative nature of quiz creation.

    Advantages. Interns are cheap and may jump at the chance to create marketing quizzes for your company.

    Disadvantages. Interns don’t necessarily understand the nuances of your target market and may be more inclined to create quizzes that resonate with their peers rather than your target audience.

    Have a Qwizard build your quiz

    If you don’t have the time, creativity, or desire to create your own quizzes, one of the best options available is to have a Qwizard build a quiz for you. Qwizards are just that: quiz whizzes! QZZR can recommend several agencies that specialize in creating viral quizzes.

    Advantages. Digital agencies understand how to create effective quizzes that get results. They have the resources to commit to a regular publishing schedule. They bring a high level of expertise to the process.

    Disadvantages. There is a cost to using a digital agency to create quizzes for you. That said, there are also costs associated with creating your own quiz content, delegating it to an assistant, or putting it in the hands of an inexperienced intern. On the other hand, a professionally developed quiz may be far more successful at generating leads, driving traffic, or generating sales than the alternatives.

    In the end, remember that creating a marketing quiz isn’t overly difficult, but to do it right, it takes time, creativity, and commitment.
