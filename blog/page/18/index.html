  • Qzzr Answers: Images and Qzzr

    Josh Little · April 3, 2014

    We work really hard to make your quizzes look beautiful. The power of images can turn a plain looking quiz into a rich, visual bonanza very quickly. We receive lots of questions about the use if images in Qzzr. This post will hopefully answer some of those questions and educate you a little on the proper use of images for creative works.

    To save you some time, here is the bottom line – the use of images in Qzzr is no different than the use of images anywhere else on the web or in creative works – you need to either own the image or have license to use it.

    For a little more information, read on.

    If I find an image on a Google image search, can I use it?

    Maybe.  Google Images is a powerful search tool and can display images from anywhere on the web.  Just because an image can be found on the internet, does not mean it is free to copy and use.  Luckily, Google has made it super easy to sort by usage rights (see below).

    What if I’m not using the quiz for commercial purposes?

    It still doesn’t matter. You must have permission or license to use an image for any creative work. There are, for certain circumstances, guidelines for the fair use of images or copyrighted works without needing to gain permission from the creator. There are a couple helpful articles on fair use on Wikipedia and the U.S. Copyright Office.

    Can I just attribute the image to the creator or source?

    Sure, but that still doesn’t make it legal. Giving credit through attribution does avoid plagiarism, but it still does not give you legal permission unless the license says so.

    What if I make changes to the image?

    Making changes to an image does not make it yours. If you do not own the rights to the image, there is no amount of changes you can make that will make it yours.

    Is Qzzr liable for my use of images?

    Nope. We cannot accept liability as it’s impossible for us to know if you have permission or not. We make it clear in our Terms of Service that we forbid the use of posting any images that do not have permission from the owner. We deal with copyright infringement accordance to the Digital Millenium Copyright Act.

    So, where can I find good images?

    There are millions of legal and free images that can be found on the web. Just search for “royalty free images” or “creative commons images” or use the Google images search tools to find the right usage rights for your needs. They may not have the exact image of a clown juggling chainsaws while eating an apple, but here are a few sources that we’ve found to help you along your quest.
