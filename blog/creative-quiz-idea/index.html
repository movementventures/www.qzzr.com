This Creative Quiz Was a Touchdown for CollegeHumor

John Williams · December 12, 2014
Case Studies Viral Marketing
Shares

You’ve likely encountered your fair share of “Which Disney/Star Wars/Game of Thrones/Hunger Games/Fill-in-the-blank Character are You?” quizzes. These quizzes are indeed popular, but they are not your only choice. Another popular quiz type is the compare and contrast quiz.

Examples of Compare and Contrast Quizzes

What do we mean by compare and contrast quiz? Let’s take a look at a couple creative quizzes that use this technique as an example:

NFL Player or Key & Peele Character – Key & Peele is a sketch comedy show starring Keegan-Michael Key and Jordan Peele. Their characters often had unusual names — as do many NFL players. This compare and contrast quiz asks questions such as:

As the person taking the quiz, it’s up to you to figure out if the person in question is an NFL player or a Key & Peele character. This quiz is fun because some of the names are so unusual you think they couldn’t possibly be real. Surprisingly, seemingly fake names are real.

Game of Thrones Scene or Creed Video – Both Game of Thrones and Creed videos feature dark and lavish scenery, making it difficult for even the most devoted of fans to distinguish between actual Game of Thrones scenes or a Creed video. Not only is this compare and contrast quiz fun because it appeals to fans of either franchise, it is also highly visual. It puts your knowledge to the test and challenges what you thought you knew you in a non-threatening manner. It also draws a parallel to the two different elements that you may not have noticed before.

Benefits of Compare and Contrast Quizzes

Compare and contrast quizzes offer several benefits:

  • Wide appeal – These online quizzes can appeal to two diverse groups as well. For example, NFL fans may have never heard of Key & Peele, yet they’ll likely check out the quiz. The same is true of the Game of Thrones Scene versus Creed Video quiz. Not only does this quiz tap into the huge Game of Thrones fan base, it also appeals to rockers.
  • Potential to go viral – Whether you scored poorly or aced the quiz, you’ll want to share it with your friends who share your passion for either Games of Thrones or Creed simply based on the subject matter alone. If you aced the quiz, of course you’ll want to show off. If you didn’t do so well, sharing a low score on an NFL Player vs. Key & Peele quiz isn’t nearly as embarrassing as flunking the US citizenship test.
  • Easy to write once you have the topic – Once you have the topic figured out, these quizzes practically write themselves. For example, let’s say you want to write a compare and contrast quiz such as “Fictional Hero or Real Life Hero.” Once you have that topic nailed, all you need to do is look up lists of both types of heroes and present them in your compare and contrast quiz. For example, John McClane (fictional) and John McCain (real life).
