]]> You know them, and although you say they are silly….you secretly LOVE them, those insanely viral outcome quizzes that you see all over the internet and in your social channels from sites like the NYTimes, BuzzFeed, and Zimbio.  Did you know the #1 article on the NYTimes last year was a quiz?  And it launched on December 21st, so it only had 10 days to gain that status.

These fun quiz questions are blowing up right now, but it’s nothing new.  It’s a basic human need to seek to understand ourselves.  This need can be met for fun and trivial ways like “What dysfunctional TV family should you belong to?” or more serious selection tools like “If you were a coffee, what kind of coffee would you be?”   As with anything on the web, it boils down to great content, but with content as a constant, the social lift on these outcome-based quizzes has been astronomical.  We’re seeing share rates hovering around the high sixty percent range on some of our most viral quizzes.  That’s just insane!  With Qzzr, you can create the same sort of viral results for your own site, brand, or blog.

How to make a great outcome based quiz.

Content is king and with quizzes that rule holds true.  What makes a great quiz experience?  Who do some quizzes go viral and others don’t?  What’s the difference between a quiz that has a 68% share rate and a 28% share rate?  Since quizzes are our business, we’ve done the research and found several of these aspects to be true on the most popular viral quizzes.

  • “Tell me about me” – Great quizzes let users take the “me drug” for just a couple minutes.  They ask thought provoking, personal questions.  The better the questions, the more likely the user will be to buy-in to the outcome, which means they will be more likely to share the outcome too.
  • “Make me look good” – Viral quizzes have many potential outcomes, but all of them either make the user look good or funny.  No one will share a quiz outcome that they aren’t proud of.
  • Relevant topics (duh) – the topic, title, content, and outcome are super relevant to the channel they are found in.  The more niche the topic and channel, the more likely the outcome will be shares.
  • They are fun – Great quizzes use humor to keep users smiling all the way to the end.   They do this with either great writing or interesting images.
  • Visually stimulating – the most viral quizzes have great visuals.  Qzzr allows tons of latitude in the use of images from animated GIF’s to transparent PNG’s

How do you create the content?

We’ve made many of our own quizzes and watched others.  While everyone has their own process, it usually follows some version of this flow.

(often the first few steps are done on a scratch pad or a doc outside of Qzzr, which makes for faster copy/paste work when building the quiz)

1. Determine your topic and title for the audience you’ll be creating the quiz for.  We’ve seen that the title of the quiz plays a massive role in the conversion from visits to quiz starts.  Popular titles for outcome quizzes are “What _____ am I?” or “What kind of _____ are you?”  or “What ______ should you _____?”

2. Create the possible outcomes.  You’ll want to create enough outcomes so that when this gets shared on social media channels, people see various results and wonder what they are.

3.  Determine the qualities that would differentiate the outcomes from one another.  This can be done on a scratch pad while doing research.  Here are the notes from a “What tech entrepreneur are you” quiz we recently created.  Notice that this is not an exhaustive list of qualities, but you can start to see some themes that could be used to differentiate – Male or female?  School of choice?  Dropout or not?

4. Craft the questions.  The questions really fall out of the research that you’ve done to differentiate the outcomes.  Not all aspects of differentiation or interesting or appropriate, but you definitely want your respondents to feel sufficiently assessed.   While asking which baby’s face is cuter is a fun question, it does beg the question “what does this have to do with what job I should have and will I really trust the results?”

5.  Source or create the images.  Great quizzes are visually stunning.  Qzzr makes that easy, but it does take some time and creativity to end up with a great result.  Here is a quiz that shows you some of the possibilities of what you can do with images if you get creative.

6. Build the quiz in Qzzr.  This is the easy part.  We’ve removed all friction from the process.  Once the content is created it should be very quick to copy and paste content and drag and drop images.

Carve out questions – you can use images to create buttons

]]> Test.  Exam.  Quiz.  Educational assessment.  How do those words make you feel?

A number is a powerful thing.  It can be used to give exact measurement.  It can say what 1000 words can’t.  It can easily be compared to other numbers.  It can also be used to beat people over the head.  Somewhere along the way, the idea of feedback in the learning process became punitive.

A relevant human experience

Seeking feedback is a profoundly relevant human experience.  If our mere existence as humans is to progress, then feedback is what makes that existence relevant.   We are constantly seeking feedback to understand our surroundings.

Is this safe? What time do I need to wake up?  How long will it take me to brush my teeth?  Does he like me?  Why are they looking at me like that?  Can I play this song again without a making a mistake?  Have I done my good deed for the day?

Feedback is a necessary and natural part of growth.  Therefore, feedback is a necessary part of learning.

Growth doesn’t happen in a vacuum.

Everything happens in relation to its context.  You can’t understand growth unless you can relate it to or measure it against something.

How do you know if you’ve grown taller?  You can’t feel it.  You can see it in a mirror or picture, but can’t tell unless you relate it to something.  These pants look too short.  I’m taller than my sister now.  I can now see on top of the refrigerator….yuck…let’s clean it.

To understand growth in learning we must have feedback just like to understand the growth of our body we must have a mark on the wall.  It’s all feedback.  It’s all necessary.

Feedback is not evil, evil is.

The question is….how do we feel about it?  Do we get nervous when we step up to the wall to measure ourselves?  Do we agonize for days in advance?  Do we resent the lower marks on the wall?  Of course not.  In fact, it’s just the opposite.  We’re eager to see how we’ve grown.  We’re proud of progress.  We’re completely satisfied by looking at those lower marks because they tell us how far we’ve come.

Why doesn’t assessment in the learning process feel as rewarding as measuring our height on the wall?

It does.

“Wait…no it doesn’t.  I get physically sick with sweaty palms when I have to take a test.”

Aha!  Why?  Let’s explore this.  This is getting good.  Think for a moment.  What are you good at?  What do you like learning about?  What do you spend your time doing?

Do you have a few topics?

Now….what if someone started asking you questions about those things?  How would that feel?  Would you enjoy that conversation?  Absolutely.  It would be invigorating talking about things you love or are passionate about.  In fact, you would probably know all of the answers to just about any question on the topic.

What if that person asking the questions was keeping track of how many questions you could answer correctly and telling you how impressed they were along the way?  Would that bother you?  Not at all.  It would feel good.

What if sent a link to a quiz on one of your favorite topics?  Would you be nervous to take it?  Not at all.  You would probably stop what you were doing immediately and take it.  It would be fun.

Aren’t those examples above some form of assessment, just like an exam, test, or quiz?

So why do some forms of assessment make you physically sick and other forms excite you feel good?  Because feedback is not evil, evil is.  Feedback is something necessary that we crave.  We seek it out.  We thrive on it.  And sometimes, we even enjoy it.  Any negative feelings around feedback come from evils perpetuated by a system or our own personal baggage.  More on this later.

How do we make all assessment useful, instructive, and enjoyable?  It has everything to do with agency and the power of questions.  And that would be an excellent topic for our next blog post.  Stay tuned.

When we started Movement, a hatchery for great ideas, we really expected that our first product would be placed somewhere outside of the world of online learning since Josh’s last two companies have been in that space.  We started with over 70 ideas or problem spaces that we wanted to explore.  As we began validating ideas and testing markets we explored physical products, ecommerce company concepts, gas stations, and recruiting apps, but there was this one idea that kept suggesting to us that there was something left to be said and we needed to say it.  Qzzr.

Why quizzes?

It’s really more about feedback than quizzes specifically.  We feel like something needs to be said about feedback in the learning process and how to measure progress.  The idea of assessment has been given a bad rap by people who have the wrong idea.  It’s become punitive, not additive.  So, we’re standing up for feedback.  Feedback is a natural human desire and an inescapable part of the learning process.  The right feedback can encourage learners to dive deeper and illuminate the path for teachers and stakeholders.  And like any great teacher, we’re starting in a place everyone understands and aim to take them on the journey with us.   More on this later.

Our aim

We aim to show the world what learning feedback needs to look like.  Along that journey, we will provide the world’s simplest and most flexible tool for doing so.  It will always work on any device.  It will always be beautiful, wildly shareable, secure,  and dead simple to use.  It will never require special software or downloads.  There will always be a robust free version that anyone can use as well as a premium version for those who need special features.

This is just the beginning.  A starting place.  We invite you on this journey along with us to provide the us feedback we need to provide you the feedback you need.
